Kozhikode

   Also known as Calicut, this is a popular tourist destination. If you are badly looking for an escapade from the hustle-bustle of the city life, you can choose Kozhikode without a second thought. This subtle city with sublime beauty, rich culture and heritage is indeed a heaven on earth. Enjoy sun-kissed beaches, family picnics, heritage sites, seafood and much more at Kozhikode in Kerala.


Best places to visit

Kappad Beach

This historically significant tourist spot is located at close proximity to Kozhikode. The aligned palm trees, decked fishing boats and beautiful scenery have earned immense popularity to the beach. It is such a soothing place that offers peace of mind and tranquillity. People from around the state head to this place to escape from the bustling city region and it is also one of the romantic beaches in Kerala.

Beypore

Indulge in the quaint beauty of an ancient port atmosphere at Kozhikode. Known as the second largest port in Kerala, it is a famous tourist-friendly attraction. Though the place served as a trading hub and ship making spot, currently the port acts as a busy tourist spot.

Peruvannamuzhi Dam

The Peruvannamuzhi Dam, set amidst the backdrop of majestic Western Ghats, is a breath-taking picnic spot. This area forms a part of the Malabar Wildlife Sanctuary and is home to several wild animals. The beautiful dam site is situated 60 km from Kozhikode town. The place is also known for a Wild Animal Rehabilitation Centre, Bird Sanctuary and Crocodile Farm.

Regional Science Centre And Planetarium

The Regional Science Centre and Planetarium in Kozhikode, Kerala, plays an important role in the tourism of the region. It has a seating capacity of 250 people at once. Sarovaram Bio Park

Venture out into this eco-friendly development area which is located next to the Canoly Canal near Kottooli. You will witness beautiful mangrove forests, wetlands and different habitats of birds here. This huge protected area of 200 acres is a safe paradise for at least 34 species of birds and 30 mangrove species. To sum up, this place is a must-visit spot for all the wildlife admirers at Kozhikode in Kerala
